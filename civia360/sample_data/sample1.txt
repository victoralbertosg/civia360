CONSTRUCTION PERMITS AND LICENSES - MUNICIPAL GUIDE
=====================================================

PROCEDURE TO OBTAIN A CONSTRUCTION LICENSE

A construction license is the official document that authorizes individuals or companies
to build, expand, modify, repair, or demolish any property within the municipality.
Building without this document is illegal and subject to penalties.

STEP 1 - SUBMIT APPLICATION AT THE OFFICE
The property owner or legal representative must visit the Urban Development Office (DDU)
at Av. Hidalgo No. 100, Ground Floor, and submit the official application form
filled in block letters.

STEP 2 - REQUIRED DOCUMENTS
The following documents must be presented in original and copy:
  1. Property deed registered with the Public Property Registry.
  2. Valid official identification of the owner (national ID, passport, or professional ID).
  3. Architectural plans signed by a certified Project Manager (DRO) with a valid license.
  4. Plumbing and electrical installation plans.
  5. Structural design and calculation report for constructions larger than 60 sqm.
  6. Proof of property tax payment up to date.
  7. Land use permit issued by the DDU (valid for 6 months).
  8. Receipt for payment of municipal fees corresponding to the type of work.

STEP 3 - TECHNICAL REVIEW AND APPROVAL
The DDU technical staff will review the documentation within a maximum of 15 business days.
If inconsistencies are found, the applicant will be notified in writing and given
5 additional business days to correct them.

STEP 4 - FEE PAYMENT
Once the technical review is approved, the applicant will receive a payment order.
Fees are calculated based on the value of the construction according to the current schedule:
  - New residential construction: 1.5% of the construction value.
  - Extension: 1.0% of the construction value.
  - Remodeling: 0.8% of the construction value.
  - Demolition: flat fee of $500 plus $5 per sqm.

STEP 5 - LICENSE ISSUANCE
After payment, the license is issued within 3 business days.
The license is valid for 12 months, extendable for 6 additional months
upon request and payment of extension fees.

OBLIGATIONS DURING CONSTRUCTION
- The license must be displayed visibly at the construction site at all times.
- The certified Project Manager must conduct weekly supervision visits
  and sign the site logbook at least once a week.
- Any changes to the original project must be reported to the DDU and processed
  as a license modification before execution.
- Upon completion, the owner must request a Certificate of Work Completion
  to regularize the property.

PENALTIES FOR BUILDING WITHOUT A LICENSE
Building without a license or failing to comply with its conditions may result in:
  - Fines ranging from 50 to 500 Units of Measure and Update (UMA).
  - Immediate suspension of construction work.
  - Demolition of the unauthorized structure at the owner's expense.
  - Disqualification from obtaining licenses for 2 years.

OFFICE HOURS
Monday to Friday, 8:00 AM to 3:00 PM.
Contact phone: 800-123-4567 ext. 201
Email: ddu@municipio.gob.mx

TOTAL PROCESSING TIME
The complete process, when documentation is complete and correct, takes between
20 and 25 business days from submission to license delivery.
